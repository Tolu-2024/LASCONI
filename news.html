CAPPING AND STRAPPING OF YEAR 2022 STUDENT MIDWIVES

LASCON celebrated newly capped midwives and announced upcoming OND/HND programs. The Provost urged graduates to uphold excellence, while Dr. Omo-Ojo emphasized their role in addressing climate change's impact on maternal health. The nursing association called for government support in infrastructure, accreditation, and scholarships. Graduates are offered automatic employment, highlighting the State's commitment to the nursing profession.

LAGOS STATE COLLEGE OF NURSING INDUCTS NEW NURSE EDUCATORS

LASCON Provost, Mrs. Orenuga, urged newly inducted nurse educators to be disciplined, diligent, and cooperative. She emphasized the importance of commitment to duty and adherence to civil service rules. Mr. Olaegbe, a Director with the Ministry of Establishment and Training, guided the inductees through the civil service rules. Mrs. Owolabi, representing the Ministry of Justice, administered the Oath of Secrecy. The inductees expressed gratitude for the opportunity to serve.

Apr 2023
LASCON CONDUCTS CBT ENTRANCE EXAM FOR CANDIDATES

292 candidates sat for the Lagos State College of Nursing (LASCON) Midwifery entrance exam, held at the LASU CBT Centre. Provost Orenuga addressed the candidates, emphasizing the importance of responsible conduct and adherence to COVID-19 safety protocols. She stressed the significance of the midwifery profession and its connection to human life. The exam was monitored by representatives from various health agencies. Online applications were accepted from December 1, 2020, to January 7, 2021.
